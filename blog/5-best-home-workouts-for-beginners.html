5 Best Home Workouts for Beginners to Build Core Strength

By Gladys A.

Starting a fitness routine can feel overwhelming, especially if you think you need a membership or complex equipment to see results. The truth is, your own bodyweight is one of the most powerful and versatile tools you have.

Core strength is the foundation of all athletic movement. It supports your spine, improves your posture, and reduces lower back pain. Here are the 5 best bodyweight exercises you can perform right in your living room to build a solid foundation.

1. The Standard Plank

Planks target your entire midsection, including your rectus abdominis, obliques, and lower back. Prop yourself on your forearms and toes, keeping your body in a straight line. Hold for 30 to 60 seconds, squeezing your core and glutes.

2. Bodyweight Squats

Squats are a fundamental lower-body exercise that also engages your core stabilizers. Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, send your hips back, and lower down as if sitting in a chair. Keep your chest up and drive through your heels.

3. Bird-Dog Extensions

On all fours, extend your right arm forward and left leg back simultaneously. Hold for 2 seconds, return to the start, and swap sides. This targets your lower back stabilizers and improves coordination and balance.

4. Glute Bridges

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Lift your hips toward the ceiling, forming a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze your glutes and core at the peak, then lower down slowly.

5. Incline Push-Ups

If standard push-ups are too challenging, use a stable surface like a couch or table. Keeping your body in a tight plank line, lower your chest to the surface and push back up. This builds chest, shoulder, and triceps strength while keeping core tension.

"Consistency beats intensity every single time. Doing a simple 15-minute bodyweight routine 3 times a week will yield better long-term results than a grueling 2-hour workout you only do once a month."
